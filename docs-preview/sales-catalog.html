CNC Plan & Process Pro Demo flow & user catalog

Upload a 3D CAD file → get a machining plan and a price in under a minute. No CAM, no spreadsheet.

A one-screen story for a sales demo, and a quick how-to for new users. Colour code throughout: blue = the answer green = confirmed orange = our estimate, please confirm

The 6-step demo — tell this story in 3 minutes

  localhost:5173
1

Drop in the drawing

Open the app, drag in a STEP file (the 3D drawing every CAD system exports) or click a sample. That's the only thing the customer has to do.

Say: “You give it the 3D file the designer already sent you — no re-drawing, no CAM setup.”
2

It reads the part for you

In seconds it recognises every hole, slot, pocket and face, works out how many setups the job needs, and which tools to use — the thinking a senior programmer does by eye.

Say: “This is the estimator's brain — feature recognition, setups, tooling — happening automatically.”
Overview  ·  Strategy  ·  Estimate  ·  Route
3

See it in 3D, part by part

The whole weldment splits into its parts. Rotate the model, click any feature to highlight it. Anything you buy instead of machine (bolts, shafts) — one click marks it purchased and drops it from the cost.

Say: “It knows a 28-body weldment is really 13 different parts — and lets you exclude what you buy in.”
4

The price — up top, instantly

A clear headline price is the first thing you see, with a typical range beside it. Below, a full breakdown: material, machining, holes, welding, setups. Every rate is orange until your shop confirms it.

Say: “This is the ‘wow’ — a defensible number in under a minute, itemised so you can stand behind it.”
5

It becomes their shop

Each shop picks the machines they own, enters their real prices once, and the app remembers. Green means confirmed. They can copy a rate card to a second machine, or edit a copy of any machine (e.g. same Ace body, Siemens controller) without losing the defaults.

Say: “Every quote comes out in their numbers, not ours. Enter once, reuse forever.”
6

Hand the customer a document

One click produces a branded PDF quotation on the shop's own letterhead, a per-part Excel cost-split, an internal effort sheet, or a draft G-code starting point. Batch pricing is built in — the per-piece price drops as quantity rises.

Say: “From a 3D file to a customer-ready quote on your letterhead — the whole loop, in one tool.”
